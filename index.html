What we do
Pure software. Zero hardware. Real savings.
We don't sell electricity, and we don't sell hardware. GridPulse simply connects to the smart devices you already own — like your solar panels, heat pump, and car charger — to automatically run them when you or your neighbors produce enough electricity.
How GridPulse activates — from market price signal to hardware command
🔗

No new hardware needed

GridPulse is pure software that sits on top of the equipment you already own. We instantly link up with your current solar setup, heat pump, or car charger — no tools or messy installations needed.

🏘️

Better together than alone

Instead of just looking at one home, GridPulse connects your entire energy community. By working as a team, your neighbourhood can easily share extra solar power and stop everyone from using too much expensive energy at the exact same time.

⚖️

Storing cheap energy for everyone

Your community's electric cars, water heaters, and batteries act like one big shared battery. GridPulse automatically fills them up when power is cheap and abundant, then shares that energy when prices spike — helping every neighbour save money.

📡

Always one step ahead

GridPulse constantly watches electricity market prices and looks at the weather report, how much solar power you are making, and your daily habits. Our smart software predicts exactly what your community will need over the next two hours, preparing your appliances to save money before prices even change.

🌱

Keep your energy local

Why send your clean solar energy back to the main grid when your neighbours can use it? GridPulse automatically routes local power to the homes that need it most, helping the whole community stand on its own two feet and cut down on outside energy bills.

💶

Honest, traceable savings

We believe in complete transparency. Our easy-to-read app shows exactly how much money your own home saves, as well as the total savings for your entire community. You will always see exactly how and where your energy bills are going down.

📚 What research says about orchestrated energy communities
Controlled demand management in energy communities increases self-sufficiency by 22–40% and reduces energy costs by 20–35% compared to uncontrolled operation. Community storage sharing adds a further 15–22% self-sufficiency uplift vs. individual households acting alone.
Sources: Fleischhacker et al. (2019) — Austrian energy communities; Luthander et al. (2015) — shared PV self-consumption; Müller & Möst (2018) — demand flexibility cost reduction

🔢 How the savings are calculated — every slider has an effect
① Tariff optimization
households × annual consumption × grid price × 12%
Loads shifted to cheap grid hours. Price paid: grid price. Higher grid price → larger saving.
② PV self-consumption uplift
extra kWh self-consumed × (grid price − €0.08 feed-in)
kWh used locally instead of exported. Avoided cost = grid price. Opportunity cost = fixed feed-in tariff €0.08/kWh.
③ Community trading — sellers
traded surplus kWh × (community sell price − €0.08 feed-in)
PV households earn community sell price instead of the low feed-in tariff. Increase the sell price slider → sellers earn more.
③ Community trading — buyers
traded surplus kWh × (grid price − community buy price)
Non-PV households pay community buy price instead of the grid price. Lower buy price → buyers save more.
④ Peak demand reduction
households × €45 × (grid price ÷ €0.28)
Grid fee saving from flattening peak demand. Scales with grid price: higher price = more valuable peak avoidance.
⑤ EV smart charging
EV count × €110 × (grid price ÷ €0.28)
Value of charging EVs at cheap windows. Scales with grid price: the higher the price, the more charging smart matters.
⑥ BESS arbitrage
BESS count × €65 × (grid price ÷ €0.28)
Charge/discharge cycling against daily grid price spread. Higher price volatility → higher arbitrage value.
⑦ Heat pump load shifting
HP count × €38 × (grid price ÷ €0.28)
Pre-heat/cool during cheap periods. Scales with grid price.
⑧ GridPulse subscription (cost)
households × €15/month × 12 = households × €180/year
Deducted from gross savings to give net savings. Shown as negative (−) in results.
Calculation basis: Tariff optimization: households × consumption × grid price × 12%. PV self-consumption uplift: extra kWh × (grid price − €0.08 feed-in); +30pp uplift from Fleischhacker et al. (2019), Austrian energy communities. Community trading sellers: traded kWh × (community sell price − €0.08). Community trading buyers: traded kWh × (grid price − community buy price). Traded kWh = PV yield × (1−scGP) × 50%. Peak demand, EV, BESS, HP savings each scale proportionally with grid price relative to €0.28 reference. GridPulse subscription: €15/household/month (€180/yr) deducted. CO₂: 0.295 kg/kWh. Estimates only; actual results vary.
